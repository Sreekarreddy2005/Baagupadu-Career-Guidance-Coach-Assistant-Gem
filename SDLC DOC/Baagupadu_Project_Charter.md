## 1.3 Executive Summary

**Baagupadu** is an AI-powered personalized mentoring ecosystem designed to help individuals—particularly **Gen Z and Millennials aged 16–30**—move past the **"Information Paradox,"** where unlimited access to content paradoxically produces decision paralysis, shallow learning, and a lack of career clarity rather than confidence and direction.

At the center of the product is **Sahayam**, a conversational AI mentor with a deliberately engineered personality blend:

- **40%** – Close Friend  
- **25%** – Mentor  
- **20%** – Curious Explorer  
- **10%** – Career Coach  
- **5%** – Storyteller  

Sahayam guides users through a structured **six-phase discovery journey**—from initial trust-building through childhood, teenage, and adult life-stage exploration, culminating in synthesized insights and a personalized guidance plan.

This Project Charter authorizes a **6-week roadmap** structured around two non-negotiable milestones:

### Milestones

| Milestone | Timeline | Objective |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **M1** | Week 4 | Working Gemini Gem prototype |
| **M2** | Week 6 | User validation and launch readiness |

- **Core Team Size:** 2–3 members
- **Development Approach:** AI-assisted development using modern GenAI tools and rapid prototyping methodologies

## SECTION 2: PROJECT Description 

### 2.2 Problem Statement — The Career Clarity Crisis

Despite unprecedented access to educational content and career resources, Gen Z in India is experiencing a career clarity crisis that manifests as burnout, disengagement, and anxiety. This is not a character flaw — it is a structural failure 

Key Research Findings:

| Metric | Finding | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **India workplace burnout rate** | 59% of employees (ages 18-24 hardest hit) vs. global avg 20% | *McKinsey Health Institute* |
| **Students with career counselling access** | Only 10.4% of Classes 9-12 students have professional counselling | *Bharat Career Aspirations Report 2025* (UN-backed, 21,000+ students) |
| **Gen Z worried AI will replace their roles** | 45% believe AI could replace them within 3-5 years | *Great Place to Work India 2025* |
| **Students without career role models** | 53.5% reported no career role model (55.8% for free school meal eligible) | *ISE Gen Z Careers Report* (1,100+ survey responses) |
| **Students stressed about future careers** | 69% say thinking about future careers causes stress (73% for girls) | *ISE Gen Z Careers Report* |

#### The Root Cause 
Students who enter careers through external pressure — family expectation, institutional prestige, or assumption of security — are far more likely to disengage early. When work does not align with identity, exhaustion becomes existential . Career counselling at 16 prevents the crisis at 25 .



### 2.2 Business Case — The Gap We Are Closing

| Metric | Current State | Target State | Source |
|--------|--------------|--------------|--------|
| Career counselling access (students) | 10.4% | 80%+ | Bharat Career Aspirations Report |
| Students with career role models | 46.5% have none | 80%+ | ISE Report |
| Gen Z with structured guidance | Limited | Accessible | Mentoria / ISE Research |

**The Opportunity:** Structured career counselling is not about telling young people what to do. It is about giving them self-knowledge and information to make their own determination before consequences accumulate . Students who go through structured aptitude mapping, interest assessment, and career exploration carry their choices with conviction and resilience

**Target demographic indicators (ages 16–30):**

- 80%+ feel overwhelmed by available learning and career resources
- 70%+ start setting goals but fail to complete them
- 65%+ lack clarity on their career direction
- 60%+ possess knowledge but cannot apply it effectively

### 4.2 The Six-Phase Journey

| Phase | Exchanges | Focus | Key Output |
|:---:|:---:|---|---|
| **1 — Trust Building**   | 3–5   | Warm welcome, psychological safety            | User comfort, engagement          |
| **2 — Childhood (0–12)** | 8–12  | Family, curiosity, play, early patterns        | Core emotional blueprint          |
| **3 — Teenage (13–19)**  | 10–15 | Identity, friends, values, resilience          | Social & values profile           |
| **4 — Adult (20–30)**    | 8–12  | Career, skills, purpose, current state         | Ambition & growth patterns        |
| **5 — Synthesis**        | 5–8   | Pattern weaving, insight sharing               | Comprehensive persona             |
| **6 — Guidance Delivery**| 5–10  | Career direction, action steps                 | Personalized roadmap              |

> Each phase builds on the prior one — the system intentionally avoids a "survey" feel by pacing the journey across short, conversational exchanges rather than a single long-form questionnaire.
